Visit Permit

A tourist visa lets individuals visit a country for leisure or family visits, typically requiring a valid passport, financial proof, and return tickets. It does not permit work during the stay.

A Visitor Visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is an official document that allows you to enter Canada as a temporary resident. Visitors can stay for up to 6 months, subject to discretion by border officers.

Criteria:
  • Valid travel document (e.g., passport)
  • Purpose: Tourism, family visit, or business
  • Proof of ties to home country
  • Financial means for your stay
Process:
  1. Check Visa Requirements
  2. Prepare Your Application
  3. Submit Application
  4. Wait for Decision
  5. Receive a Decision
  6. Travel to Canada

Required Documents:

  • Valid Passport
  • Completed Application Form
  • Proof of Financial Support
  • Travel Itinerary
  • Ties to Home Country
  • Photographs
  • Application Fees
Frequently Asked:
  • Q1: How long can I stay?
    A: Up to 6 months.
  • Q2: Do I need a visa?
    A: It depends on your nationality.
  • Q3: Can I work?
    A: No, not with a Visitor Visa.
  • Q4: What happens if I overstay?
    A: You may face penalties or be denied future entry.
  • Q5: Can I extend my stay?
    A: Yes, but you must apply before your visa expires.
  • Q6: Are biometrics required?
    A: Yes, for some applicants depending on nationality.
  • Q7: Does the visa allow multiple entries?
    A: It depends on the type of visa issued.
