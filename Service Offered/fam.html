Family Permit

Family sponsorship allows individuals to bring their family members to live with them in another country, requiring proof of financial stability and support commitment. It helps reunite families and provides opportunities for a better life.

Explore

Family Sponsorship is a program that allows Canadian citizens and permanent residents to sponsor certain family members to immigrate to Canada. The goal is to help families reunite and build their lives together in Canada.

Criteria:

Eligibility of the Sponsor:
  • Canadian citizen or permanent resident.
  • At least 18 years old.
  • Must financially support the sponsored person.
  • Not receiving social assistance (except for disability).
Eligibility of the Sponsored Person:
  • Spouse, partner, or dependent child under age 22.
  • Parents or grandparents (with conditions).
  • Must meet health and security requirements.
Process:
  1. Determine eligibility for both sponsor and applicant.
  2. Submit sponsorship application to IRCC.
  3. Wait for IRCC review and processing.
  4. If approved, obtain permanent residency.
  5. Arrive in Canada as a permanent resident.
Required Documents:
  • For the Sponsor: Proof of status, income, application form.
  • For the Sponsored: Identity, relationship, police clearance, medical.
  • For Both: Fees, photos, declaration forms.
Frequently Asked:
  • Q1: Can I bring my family?
    A: Yes, eligible dependents can accompany you.
  • Q2: Do I need a job offer?
    A: Not for sponsorship, but financial proof is needed.
  • Q3: Can I apply outside Canada?
    A: Yes, applications from abroad are accepted.
