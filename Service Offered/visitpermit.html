A tourist visa lets individuals visit a country for leisure or family visits. It does not permit work during the stay.

About

A Visitor Visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is an official document that allows you to enter Canada as a temporary resident. Visitors can stay for up to 6 months, subject to discretion by border officers.

Criteria

  • Valid travel document (e.g., passport)
  • Purpose: Tourism, family visit, or business
  • Proof of ties to home country
  • Financial means for your stay

Step By Step Process

  1. Check Visa Requirements
  2. Prepare Your Application
  3. Submit Application
  4. Wait for Decision
  5. Receive a Decision
  6. Travel to Canada

Required Documents

  • Valid Passport
  • Completed Application Form
  • Proof of Financial Support
  • Travel Itinerary
  • Ties to Home Country
  • Photographs
  • Application Fees

FAQs

1. How long can I stay? Up to 6 months.

2. Do I need a visa? It depends on your nationality.

3. Can I work? No, not with a Visitor Visa.

4. Overstaying? May lead to penalties.

5. Can I extend? Yes, before expiration.

6. Biometrics? Required for some applicants.

7. Multiple entries? Depends on the visa type issued.
