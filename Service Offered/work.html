Work Permit

A work permit allows individuals to work legally in a specific country, often requiring employer sponsorship or meeting eligibility criteria. For caregivers, additional requirements may include qualifications and a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA).

Explore

A work permit is required for foreign skilled workers wishing to work temporarily in Canada. To qualify, a person must have a temporary offer of employment from a Canadian employer. Certain positions may require knowledge of the National Occupation Classification (NOC) Code, which classifies employment types by Canadian standards. Individuals applying for a Permanent Resident (PR) card may also apply for an open work permit.

Criteria:

There are two main types of work permits in Canada:

  • Open Work Permit (OWP):
    • Not job-specific
    • No employer required when applying
    • Includes permits for spouses, post-graduation work, youth programs, and more
  • Employer-Specific Work Permit:
    • Tied to a specific employer, job duration, and location
    • Conditions must be followed as stated in the permit
Process:
  1. Employer Applies for LMIA:
    • May require an LMIA to prove the need for a foreign worker.
  2. Employer Extends a Job Offer:
    • Once LMIA is approved, a job offer is extended.
  3. Worker Applies for Work Permit:
    • The worker applies after receiving the offer.
  4. Work Permit Issued:
    • If criteria are met, the permit is issued.
Required Documents:
  • For the Sponsor:
    • Proof of citizenship or permanent residency
    • Proof of income
    • Completed application
    • Divorce or death certificates (if applicable)
  • For the Sponsored Person:
    • Proof of relationship
    • Proof of identity
    • Medical exam results
    • Police certificates
    • Proof of dependency (if any)
  • For Both:
    • Application fee receipt
Frequently Asked:
  • Q1: Can I apply without a job offer?
    A: In most cases, no. Open work permits are exceptions.
  • Q2: What’s the difference between permits?
    A: Open permits are not job-specific. Employer-specific are restricted.
  • Q3: How long can I stay?
    A: Depends on job offer and permit type.
  • Q4: Can my family come with me?
    A: Yes. Spouses may get a permit; children can study.
  • Q5: How can I succeed?
    A: Submit accurate docs. Consider an immigration consultant.
